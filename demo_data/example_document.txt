# Example Research Note: Dog Training Basics

Positive reinforcement is the most effective method for training dogs.
Studies show that dogs learn faster when rewarded with treats rather than
punished.

## Key Concepts

1. Operant conditioning, first described by B.F. Skinner in 1938, is the
   foundation of modern animal training. Subjects learn to associate
   actions with consequences.

2. Clicker training bridges the gap between behavior and reward using a
   distinct marker sound. The click marks the exact moment of correct
   behavior, eliminating ambiguity in communication.

3. Shaping rewards successive approximations of the target behavior.
   Instead of waiting for perfection, trainers reward small steps.

## Research Findings

A study by Dr. Claudia Fugazza at Eotvos Lorand University in Budapest
(2020) demonstrated that subjects can learn and retain up to 12 new words
per week using systematic shaping techniques.

Functional MRI studies found that the caudate nucleus activates in
anticipation of rewards, suggesting positive emotional states during
training sessions.

## Common Mistakes

- Inconsistent timing of rewards (the number one beginner error)
- Using too many new commands simultaneously
- Punishing instead of redirecting unwanted behaviors
- Ignoring stress signals during training

## Recommended Protocol

Start with 2-3 high-frequency words. Practice in short 5-minute sessions,
3-4 times daily. Always end on a success. Never train when frustrated.
